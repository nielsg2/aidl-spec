# Provenance & Open-Publication Statement

## Origin

AIDL began as a personal effort to make AI assistants behave predictably, then
grew into a model for governing multiple agents. Its development is recorded in
independent, timestamped version-control history:

- **2025-06-08** — the directive framework first appears under version control
  (initial `AI_Directives` document).
- **2025-06-19 → 06-23** — rapid iteration through a versioned directive series.
- **2025-06-25** — the `.aidl` compact directive language is named and given its
  own structure.

These dates are corroborated across two independent platforms (a source-control
host and a separate conversational-AI history), which agree to within the hour on
the founding day.

This repository is a **curated public extract** of that larger private working
set. It contains only the general, reusable specification and governance model;
project-specific, personal, and unrelated material has been deliberately left out.
That is why this repository's own creation date is later than the origin dates
above — the *work* is from 2025; this *clean release* is the public form of it.

## On independent / parallel development

AIDL was not developed in isolation from the field, nor does it claim primacy.
Through 2025–2026, many independent efforts converged on machine-readable runtime
governance for AI agents — academic domain-specific languages, industry risk
taxonomies, and vendor toolkits among them; some predate this work, most followed
it. None copied the others. The shared cause is simple: autonomous agents without
governance are a liability, and the field reached that realization at roughly the
same time.

That convergence is the reason for publishing openly.

## Why this is published openly

This is released as a **contribution and as prior art**, under the MIT license —
not as a patent claim. An idea whose time has come is better contributed to the
commons than fenced off. Publishing openly:

- puts the work in the public record under its author's name,
- keeps the underlying ideas free for anyone to build on, and
- is consistent with the belief that governing AI responsibly should not be
  anyone's private monopoly.
